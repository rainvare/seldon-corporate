Samsung Electronics entered structural fragility in Q3 2022 — 18 months before the collapse of its DS division became visible in financial statements. The model detected progressive loss of technological resilience (Rt) driven by strategic overcommitment to NAND and DRAM commodity cycles while SK Hynix captured the HBM market. By 2023, operating losses in DS reached KRW 14.88 trillion, and management issued a public apology — an event with no precedent in Samsung's corporate history.

Key finding: The crisis was not a demand shock. It was a structural resilience failure. Q(t) crossed the fragility threshold in 2022 (Q = −0.08) before the financial collapse. By 2023 it reached −0.41, the deepest deficit in the observation period. All three Early Warning Signals activated simultaneously in 2022, providing a 12-month predictive lead.
