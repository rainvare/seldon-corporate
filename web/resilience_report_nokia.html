Validation criterion (from THEORY.md §8.2): The model passes if Q crosses −0.20 before the documented crisis event in ≥ 4/6 validation cases with lead time ≥ 4 quarters. Nokia confirms the criterion with a 4-quarter lead. Framework generalization to a company not used in calibration is conditionally validated.

Nokia's collapse is the defining case of cognitive resilience failure. Unlike Samsung — where the crisis was driven by a technological shock that arrived faster than adaptation — Nokia had between 2 and 4 years of clear signal before the rupture. The iPhone arrived in 2007. Nokia's internal research acknowledged the threat. The financial resources to respond were available (Rf = 0.82). The R&D capability existed (Rt = 0.58 in 2007). What failed was the organizational capacity to process the signal and act on it — precisely what Rc measures.

The Rc finding: Nokia's highest-weight dimension (wc = 0.31) was Cognitive Resilience, derived from the structural observation that average C-suite tenure exceeded 10 years and the company had been led by the same chairman (Jorma Ollila) since 1992. The model weighted this heavily because tenure concentration predicts path dependence in crisis decision-making. That weighting proved correct — the crisis was not resource-constrained, it was governance-constrained.

Resilience Margin Q(t) — 2007 to 2013

Key result: Q crosses the Structural Rupture threshold (−0.20) in 2010 — 12 months before the "Burning Platform" memo (February 2011). The gradual slope from 2007 to 2010 is the signature of cognitive collapse: unlike Samsung's abrupt Q spike in 2023, Nokia deteriorated steadily for 3 years before crossing the threshold.
Regime zones: Q > 0.15 = Stability · 0.15 ≥ Q > −0.05 = Tension · −0.05 ≥ Q > −0.20 = Fragility · Q ≤ −0.20 = Structural Rupture. Nokia spent one year in Tension (2009) before entering Rupture in 2010. Compare Samsung: entered Tension in 2021, Rupture in 2023 — faster trajectory, less warning window.

Model Implication for Current Cases Nokia demonstrates that Rf does not save companies from Rc failures. Having the financial capacity to act and failing to act are equivalent outcomes once the rupture threshold is crossed. The model's weighting of Rc at 0.31 — the highest weight for Nokia, derived from structural tenure data — correctly identified the constraint. This validates the fifth dimension's inclusion in the framework. The four-dimension version (without Rc) would have predicted Nokia's crisis but could not have identified the intervention lever.
